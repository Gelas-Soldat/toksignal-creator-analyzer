CSV guide

Use the template when TikTok gives you a TXT or JSON account export instead of a clean creator analytics CSV. The analyzer works best with one row per video. You can also use Paste analytics when you copy rows from TikTok Studio, Excel, Google Sheets, or any table with headers.

From the TikTok app

  1. Open TikTok and go to Profile.
  2. Tap Menu, then Settings and privacy.
  3. Tap Account, then Download your data.
  4. Request the file, download it when ready, then move the useful video metrics into the CSV template.

From web or desktop

  1. Open TikTok in a browser and check TikTok Studio or Analytics.
  2. Look for video performance data and export options if available for your account.
  3. If no CSV export is visible, download the template here and manually paste the post metrics.
  4. Upload the completed CSV into this analyzer.

Recommended columns:

  • caption, date, views, likes, comments, shares, saves, duration
  • Alternative names like video title, post date, play count, and favorites are detected too.

For the cleanest report, include at least 20 videos.

Paste analytics data

Paste copied rows from TikTok Studio, Excel, Google Sheets, Notion, or a CSV file. Include a header row so the analyzer can detect the columns.

Best formatRows with headers like caption, date, views, likes, comments, shares, saves, and duration.
